About Me

I am a bioinformatician with experience in genomic data analysis, molecular diagnostics, and multi-omics integration. I am passionate about applying machine learning and AI to biological data, particularly in infectious diseases and neurodegenerative disorders. I have worked across academia and industry, from rare disease and cancer genomics at Genomics England to malaria population genomics at MRC Unit The Gambia (LSHTM). My work bridges computational pipelines and biological insight, with a strong focus on reproducibility and collaborative problem-solving.

Selected Projects

Adaptive Sampling Variant Calling in Cancer Panels

Developed a Nextflow pipeline to compare Oxford Nanopore adaptive sampling with whole-genome sequencing for variant detection in 678 COSMIC cancer panel genes. Achieved high concordance in SNV and SV detection, with added methylation profiling capabilities. Clinically confirmed a complex heterozygous deletion in the EYS gene.

Wastewater SARS-CoV-2 Genomic Surveillance

Validated ONT sequencing for wastewater-based SARS-CoV-2 variant monitoring in collaboration with UKHSA. Compared Illumina and ONT platforms, using custom pipelines for variant detection and demixing. Demonstrated ONT’s feasibility for real-time genomic surveillance in remote settings.

Plasmodium Population Genomics

Analyzing whole-genome and transcriptomic data of Plasmodium species to investigate population structure, selective sweeps, and drug resistance. Integrates genomic data with epidemiological and microbiome profiles to understand malaria dynamics across Africa and Southeast Asia.

Machine Learning for Single-Cell Multi-omics in Alzheimer’s Disease

Developing an ML pipeline integrating scRNA-seq and scATAC-seq to classify immune cell subtypes and map Alzheimer’s disease risk variants to cell-type-specific regulatory elements. Utilizes XGBoost and logistic regression for predictive modeling.
